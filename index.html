Text-level semantics

Citations

Only use for the titles of books, films, journal articles & plays. Don't bother for the titles of blog posts, news articles, wikipedia entries, magazine titles or anything else. Never use for the names of people or products.

Emphasis

Only use to mark words or short phrases which would be stressed when spoken aloud.

Good:

He really hated it The music was loud

Bad:

She asked the person for a ticket. In Star Wars, he had a hit.

Strong emphasis

Only use strong to mark key words (usually nouns) that are especially relevant. In practice: rarely use it.

Good:

The conference was about user-experience design. You searched for Christmas.

Bad:

She really, really loved it. Search matches: "She loved Christmas…"

Italics

Use for any cases not covered by or where being typographically-marked (typically with italics) is essential to the meaning of the text and isn't just styling. Typically 'an alternate voice'. In practice: use rarely.

Good:

It might work. It might not.

Bad:
